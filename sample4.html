Sipo Eggs Recipe

Sipo Eggs is a Filipino quail egg and shrimp dish that originated in Pampanga. This dish includes a rich, thick sauce, shrimp, quail eggs and mixed vegetables containing carrots, green peas, and corn. It is only served on exceptional occasions.

Comparison of Sipo eggs and Chicken Pastel

As a child, I would often mistake Chicken Pastel as my favorite dish, Sipo Eggs. I cannot distinguish the difference between the two dishes back then because it looks the same for me. It features a creamy sauce, chicken and carrots. However, now that I'm already a teenager, I've come to know that their difference is that, Sipo egg has of course eggs, shrimp, carrot and peas. While Chicken Pastel have, sausages, mushrooms, green and red bell pepper.

Sipo egg Versions

For a more festive and rich taste of Sipo egg you can add minced ham or luncheon meat. Another version would be using this dish as a topping for pasta but excluding the egg, and you'll get something similar to Shrimp Alfredo

Sipo Eggs Recipe

A festive and creamy taste of quail egg, mixed vegeatables, and seafood in a dish.

Course Type:Main Dish

Cuisine:Kapampangan

Keyword:Sipo Eggs, Creamt quail egg dish

Prep Time:12 minutes

Cook Time:25 minutes

Total Time:37 minutes

Serving:6

Calories:362kcal

Author: Francesca Noreen Arco

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. Shrimp
  • 20 pcs. quail egg
  • 1 pc. onion
  • 1 tbsp.garlic
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1 tbsp. cooking oil
  • 1 cup of green peas
  • 1 peace carrot
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 4 ounces ham
  • 1 can cream of mushroom
  • 1/2 cup heavy whipping cream
  • salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Melt butter and oil in a pan over medium heat. Cook the shrimp until they are done on all sides. Set aside the shrimps from the butter.
  2. Saut√© garlic and onion in the same pan with the butter until soft. Add the carrots and green peas next. If you're using canned green peas, add them at the end. Cook for 2 minutes after adding the ham. Add 1/2 cup water, cover, and cook until carrots are almost ready.
  3. Pour in the all-purpose cream and mix in the quail eggs and shrimp. Season with salt and pepper and cook for about 3 minutes over medium heat.
  4. Add the cup of heavy cream and mix for 2-3 minutes or until the sauce thickens.
  5. Serve warm and enjoy!
